01 — PID CONTROLLER
Proportional · Integral · Derivative
The PID controller is the workhorse of industrial control systems. It calculates an error signal e(t) = setpoint − measurement and applies three corrective actions. P responds to current error. I eliminates steady-state error. D predicts future error from the rate of change.
u(t) = Kp·e(t) + Ki·∫e(t)dt + Kd·de(t)/dt
U(s) = Kp·E(s) + Ki·E(s)/s + Kd·s·E(s)

P ONLY
Proportional action drives output proportional to error.
High Kp = faster response but oscillation.
Always leaves a steady-state error (offset).
I — ELIMINATES OFFSET
Integral accumulates error over time.
Drives steady-state error to zero.
Too high Ki → integral windup and oscillation.
D — PREDICTS FUTURE
Derivative acts on rate of change of error.
Dampens oscillations, improves stability.
Sensitive to sensor noise — use carefully.

02 — STEP RESPONSE
Second-Order System Response
Most physical systems can be approximated as second-order. The step response reveals fundamental system characteristics: rise time, settling time, overshoot, and steady-state error. Damping ratio ζ determines the character of the response.
G(s) = ωn² / (s² + 2ζωn·s + ωn²)
ζ < 1: underdamped (oscillates) | ζ = 1: critically damped | ζ > 1: overdamped

03 — BODE PLOT
Frequency Response: Magnitude & Phase
The Bode plot shows how a system responds to sinusoidal inputs at different frequencies. Magnitude (gain in dB) tells how much the signal is amplified or attenuated. Phase tells how much the output lags the input. Critical stability margins are read directly from the Bode plot.
G(jω) = K / (jω(jωτ + 1))  [Type 1 system]
|G(jω)|dB = 20·log₁₀|G(jω)|    ∠G(jω) = -90° - arctan(ωτ)

04 — ROOT LOCUS
Poles, Zeros and Closed-Loop Stability
The root locus shows how the closed-loop poles move in the s-plane as gain K increases from 0 to ∞. Poles in the left half-plane (LHP) = stable. Poles in the right half-plane (RHP) = unstable. The imaginary axis is the stability boundary.
Closed-loop: T(s) = KG(s) / (1 + KG(s))
Characteristic eq: 1 + KG(s)H(s) = 0    Poles: 1 + K·N(s)/D(s) = 0

A system is BIBO stable if all closed-loop poles have negative real parts.
